Parameters


  1. Teaching Learning And Resources


    • Faculty Qualifications and Experience: Assess the qualifications, experience, and expertise of the faculty members in various disciplines.
    • Teaching Pedagogy: Evaluate the teaching methods employed, including lectures, practical sessions, seminars, and the integration of technology.
    • Curriculum Design: Examine the relevance, currency, and comprehensiveness of the curriculum in meeting educational objectives and industry demands.
    • Student-Teacher Ratio: Analyze the ratio of students to teachers to ensure adequate individual attention and mentorship.
    • Infrastructure and Facilities: Assess the availability and adequacy of facilities such as classrooms, laboratories, libraries, and recreational spaces.
    • Learning Resources: Evaluate the availability and accessibility of learning resources including textbooks, journals, online databases, and educational software.
    • Assessment and Evaluation: Review the methods used for assessing student learning, including exams, projects, presentations, and their alignment with learning outcomes.

  2. Research and Professional Practice


    • Research Output: Measure the quantity and quality of research publications, including journal papers, conference proceedings, patents, and books authored by faculty members and students.
    • Research Funding: Assess the amount and sources of research funding secured by the college, including grants from government agencies, industry collaborations, and internal funding mechanisms.
    • Research Facilities: Evaluate the availability and adequacy of research facilities, laboratories, equipment, and infrastructure supporting research activities in different disciplines.
    • Collaborations and Partnerships: Examine the extent of collaborations with industries, research institutions, government agencies, and international universities for joint research projects, technology transfer, and knowledge exchange.
    • Professional Development Programs: Assess the availability and effectiveness of programs aimed at enhancing faculty and student research skills, such as workshops, seminars, conferences, and training sessions.
    • Innovation and Entrepreneurship: Measure the college's efforts in fostering innovation, entrepreneurship, and commercialization of research outcomes through incubation centers, startup support, and industry partnerships.
    • Industry Relevance: Evaluate the alignment of research activities with industry needs, technological trends, and societal challenges, ensuring the practical applicability and relevance of research outcomes.

  3. Graduation outcome


    • Employment Rate: Measure the percentage of graduates who secure employment in their field of study within a specified period after graduation.
    • Placement Statistics: Analyze the placement records, including the average salary package, companies visited for recruitment, and job roles offered to graduates.
    • Further Education Pursuit: Track the number of graduates who pursue higher studies, enroll in postgraduate programs, or opt for research or professional certifications.
    • Industry Feedback: Gather feedback from employers regarding the readiness, skills, and competencies of graduates for the workforce, including communication skills, technical proficiency, and problem-solving abilities.
    • Entrepreneurship Initiatives: Assess the number of graduates who start their own ventures, participate in entrepreneurial activities, or join startup incubation programs.
    • Alumni Success Stories: Highlight success stories of alumni, including their career progression, achievements, and contributions to their respective fields and society.

  4. Outreach & Inclusivity


    • Community Engagement Programs: Evaluate the college's involvement in outreach programs, community service initiatives, and partnerships with local organizations to address societal needs and contribute to community development.
    • Diversity in Student Body: Assess the diversity within the student population in terms of gender, ethnicity, socioeconomic background, geographic location, and other demographic factors, promoting inclusivity and diversity on campus.
    • Accessibility: Evaluate the accessibility of campus facilities, infrastructure, and educational resources for students with disabilities, ensuring equal opportunities and support for all students.
    • Scholarships and Financial Aid: Measure the availability and effectiveness of scholarships, grants, and financial aid programs aimed at supporting students from underprivileged backgrounds and promoting socioeconomic diversity.
    • Inclusive Policies and Practices: Review the institution's policies, practices, and initiatives aimed at fostering an inclusive and supportive campus environment, including anti-discrimination policies, diversity training, and cultural awareness programs.

  5. Perception


    • Academic Reputation: The perception of the academic quality and rigor of programs offered by the college, based on factors such as faculty expertise, research output, and alumni success.
    • Industry Perception: The perception of the college by employers and industry professionals regarding the relevance of its programs, the quality of graduates, and the institution's contributions to workforce development.
    • Student Satisfaction: The perception of current students regarding the overall educational experience, including teaching quality, facilities, support services, and extracurricular opportunities.
    • Alumni Feedback: The perception of alumni regarding the value of their education, career outcomes, and the impact of the college on their professional and personal development.
    • Parental Perception: The perception of parents or guardians of current or prospective students regarding the college's reputation, safety, academic environment, and future prospects for their children.
